# V4 Glossary

*Terms as used in V4 (inside-out). Where a term's meaning changed from V3, the
change is noted. The full V3 glossary is preserved in
`v3_archive/V3_glossary.md`.*

---

**Ambient renewal-flux** — The flux that renews every pattern at every tick
(Chapter 2). Gravity is a *shadow* cast in this flux (Chapter 4), not a body's own
emission. Load-bearing: without renewal there is no flux to shadow.

**Append-only** — The axiom that structure is written, never deleted. Makes time
monotonic (Ch 2), the trie permanent (Ch 6), and equilibrium unreachable.

**Bound engine** — A pattern that holds itself by consuming renewal-flux (Same) and
shedding what it cannot use (Different). Produces mass (the holding), light (the
exhaust), and gravity (the shadow). *Chapter 4.*

**Boundary-layer instrument** — The validated inside-out dimension reader: the
participation-ratio rank of the tap × tap lag-correlation matrix
(`boundary_layer_dim.py`). Passes the adversarial zoo that broke the god-view
detectors. *Chapter 3.*

**Coherence limit** — The maximum stable pattern size. Metabolism scales as volume,
exhaust sheds through surface, so the core saturates past a critical size
(R_max ∝ √(θ/p)). The framework's Eddington/Chandrasekhar bound. *Chapter 4.*

**c (speed of light)** — The substrate's sample-rate limit: the maximum rate of
change propagation. Light saturates it because, being unbound exhaust, it has no
internal clock to slow it. *Chapter 4.*

**Depth** — How many causal recursive calls a state is from the root. Time is the
order in which a pattern reads its own depth. *Chapter 2.*

**Different** — The comparison outcome where an arrival diverges from existing
structure. Physics: radiation (shed the divergence). Information: a branch point.
*Chapters 1, 4, 6.*

**Dimension (perceived)** — *Changed from V3.* Not a property of the graph but of the
reading: the lag-correlation rank an embedded observer's channels resolve. An
expander reads high; a lattice reads low. *Chapter 3.*

**Flooding-lag substrate** — The unified engine (`FloodingLagSubstrate`): a pipe
network with ambient flux flooding through it, no coordinate grid, lag = path length.
One consuming pattern on it produces gravity, perception, and a coherence limit.
*Chapter 7.*

**God-view** — The (nonexistent) outside vantage from which the whole graph is
surveyed at once. V4's central negative claim: **there is no god-view.** Smuggling one
in is the recurring cause of every stalled result. *Chapter 1; Chapter 8.*

**Gravity** — *Changed from V3.* The ambient-renewal *shadow* a consumer casts: a
force (−∇F), attractive, carrying the local dimension (1/r in 3D). "LeSage done
right." Not a body's own outflow (which pushes away). *Chapter 4.*

**Geometrogenesis** — The open problem of whether the substrate's own growth *selects*
a low-dimensional geometry. **Unsolved here** (P1d recon leans negative). The
framework's biggest wall. *Chapter 3 §3.5; Chapter 8.*

**Inside-out** — Reading the world from an embedded pattern's arrivals, never from
outside. The organizing commitment of V4. *Chapter 1.*

**Lag** — Arrival delay of deposits reaching a pattern. Distance *is* lag; space is a
rendered lag structure. Lags are frame-invariant (objective); "who moves" is not.
*Chapters 3, 5.*

**LeSage killers** — The three classical refutations of push-gravity (sign, drag,
heat), all dissolved by the one god-view→inside-out correction. *Chapter 4 §4.4.*

**Light** — The exhaust of a bound engine: the shed Different-stream. Streams at c
(§ c). *Chapter 4.*

**No-overlap axiom** — Branches never share state; the substrate is a pure functional
pipeline. "Empty space" is therefore only a rendered time-delay between deposits, not
a shared container. *Chapter 1 §1.3.*

**Observer = Renderer = Being** — Any self-re-rendering pattern is all three at once:
it exists (re-renders), observes (consuming the lag-flux is perceiving), and renders
itself to others (its shadow and exhaust are what they read). *Chapter 5.* The
observer is the **functional** comparison primitive, **not** consciousness/qualia.

**Objectivity = invariance** — With no god-view, a quantity is objective exactly
insofar as all local observers agree on it. *Chapter 5 §5.4.*

**Renewal (temporal surfing, Doc 28)** — Patterns do not persist; they re-form
themselves each tick out of the ambient flux. Identity is continuity of pattern, not
of material. The axiom the engine runs on. *Chapter 2.*

**ρ = 2.0 signature** — The quadratic source-scaling that every time-including system
shows, versus sub-quadratic (~1.5) for pure spatial dimensions. Evidence that time is
a special, amplifying generator — not a spatial axis. *Chapter 2 §2.4.*

**Same** — The comparison outcome where an arrival matches existing structure.
Physics: gravity (reincorporate). Information: retrieval / shared prefix. *Chapters
1, 4, 6.*

**Three-state alphabet** — Same / Different / Unknown; the exhaustive set of
comparison outcomes the one operation speaks. No fourth state. *Chapter 1.*

**Tick** — One causal recursive call. Not itself "time"; time is the *read* of tick
depth. *Chapters 1, 2.*

**Trie** — The prefix tree the three-state dynamics build under append-only. It *is*
the graph read as information: compression, similarity-search (= movement = gravity),
shared-prefix particle identity. *Chapter 6.*

**Unknown** — The comparison outcome on virgin substrate. Physics: expansion.
Information: learning / the frontier. The only operation that creates new structure —
and it always creates *more* frontier. *Chapters 1, 6.*
