*Coherence over orthodoxy: a model is valid if it is internally consistent, falsifiable, and explanatory — regardless of alignment with current physical dogma.*

V4 is the fourth consolidation of the tick-frame theory — and it will be
superseded too. It is not a finished theory, and there is no "final" version in

## What changed from V3

V3 (March 2026) was the graph-first consolidation: the world as a graph you look
*at*, with a geometry you *measure*. It was a real step — but it kept hitting
walls in the 118–138 gravity/geometrogenesis experiment line, and it took a long
time to see why. **The outside view does not exist in this ontology.** There is no
observer who sees the whole graph. There are only patterns *inside* it, each
reading what has arrived. Every hard problem that stalled V3 — the sign of
gravity, the measurement of dimension, "who moves" — dissolved once it was
re-derived from the inside. That reconciliation is the RAW 134–137 arc, and it is
the spine of V4.

V4 is therefore not V3-plus-chapters. It is V3 re-read from inside-out. The
mechanism (deposit / hop / extend; the three-state alphabet) survives intact.
What changes is the point of view, and with it every claim about gravity,
dimension, and the observer.

The full V3 corpus is preserved in `v3_archive/` (chapters, glossary, experiment
index). Nothing was deleted; it is the record of how we got here.

## The one honest headline

A single operation on a raw graph, **read from inside and never from a god's-eye
view**, is a candidate for producing what we observe. In toy models it clears the
three classical objections to shadow-gravity and yields an inside-out way to
measure dimension. It has **not** been shown to *select* a low-dimensional
geometry in the first place — that is the open wall (Chapter 3 §, Chapter 8).
