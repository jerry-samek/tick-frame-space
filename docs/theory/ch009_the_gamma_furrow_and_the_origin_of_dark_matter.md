## 2. The Gamma Furrow

The Gamma Furrow is the set of all events whose gamma imprints have successfully reached the observer’s spatial point by the current tick.

Formally:

> The Gamma Furrow is the observer's past light cone expressed in gamma‑field dynamics.

It is defined by:

- the observer's position
- the finite propagation speed of gamma smoothing
- the geometry of space
- the history of all imprints that intersect the observer's location

Only events inside this furrow are observable.

**Retrospective Observation**

All detection corresponds to past imprints. The present state of distant entities is fundamentally inaccessible.

**Irreducible Blindness**

Events outside the furrow are not just unseen — they are unseeable.

## 3. The Dark Matter Principle

### 3.1 Definition

In the gamma‑field ontology, dark matter is not a substance. It is the set of all gamma imprints that:

- exist in the universe
- influence other regions
- but never intersect the observer's gamma furrow

Thus:

Dark matter = all events whose imprints missed us.

This includes the overwhelming majority of all activity in the universe.

### 3.2 Geometric Interpretation

Imagine the universe as a sea of expanding imprints:

```
[imprint] ---> ---> (missed us)
\
\__ [observer]
```

Most imprints:

- propagate in other directions
- never reach our location
- never contribute to our observable field

Yet they still exist and shape the global gamma landscape.

### 3.3 Why This Appears as "Dark Matter"

From the observer's perspective:

- these imprints do not emit detectable signals
- they do not interact electromagnetically
- they do not appear in any observational channel
- but they contribute to the overall gamma curvature of the universe

This matches the phenomenology of dark matter:

- invisible
- non‑interacting
- gravitationally relevant
- vastly more abundant than visible matter

But in this ontology, no exotic particles are required.

No additional force or field is needed.
