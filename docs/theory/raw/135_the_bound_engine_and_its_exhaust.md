## Abstract

One rendering loop, followed to its consequences:

1. **A pattern holds by re-rendering itself on its own clock** — and that clock is *slower than c*, set by the pattern's extent, not by the substrate step. (This is the tick-budget γ of Exp 128 v11, restated: proper time = the pattern's internal loop length.)
2. **Holding necessarily sheds.** A self-maintaining loop cannot reincorporate everything; what falls out each cycle — depleted, spent deposits — must leave. This is not an add-on; it is Prigogine's theorem that dissipative structures export entropy ("entropy is the price of structure"). Holding *is* radiating.
3. **The exhaust streams at c, and that is what light is.** A deposit bound in the loop rides the pattern's slow clock; a deposit thrown clear of the loop has no clock of its own and reverts to the only rate left — the bare frontier rate, c. So *bound = slow (matter), unbound exhaust = fast (radiation)*, and the enormous speed gap between matter and light is this distinction, not a free parameter.
4. **The exhaust is the coupling between patterns**, and it does two opposite things at once: it **pushes** the receiver (radiation pressure, momentum — repulsive) and it is **consumed** by the receiver, feeding growth on the side it arrives from (the body drifts up-flux — attractive). Same stream, opposite signs.
5. **The crossover between them is the Eddington limit** — the point where outward radiation pressure (∝ flux) balances inward consumption-growth (∝ consumed flux). Below capacity, consumption wins: attraction, diluting as 1/r² over the frontier (the N(r) ∝ r² area-law exponent — the same exponent that makes space 3D; RAW 134 Addendum E). At/above capacity, the un-consumable excess pushes: radiation pressure. This is RAW 118's "one saturation curve, two regimes," now with a mechanism instead of an assertion.
6. **The two signs separate only for a slow pattern.** The push arrives every substrate-tick and, against a slowly-re-rendering body, averages to jitter; the consumption-drift is slow and directional and accumulates. Attraction beats the push *iff* the pattern's clock is sub-c. With no timescale separation the two never separate → shred or freeze. **The slow-clock (point 1) and the coupling (point 4) are one condition seen twice.**
7. **Consume→grow→consume-more is positive feedback = runaway** (literally the Exp 138 P1b explosion). The push at saturation is what caps it. Push and pull *together* self-regulate at the Eddington balance — and a growth rate pinned there is exactly the **comoving, polynomial-in-time, self-critical phase** RAW 134 Addendum F hunted and Exp 138 P1c failed to find. **It failed because the substrate had no exhaust at all** — the selector was pure removal (decay), no shed-and-reconsume channel, hence no regulator, hence only explosion or freeze.

The claim of the document: mass, light, the speed gap between them, the inter-mass coupling, the 1/r² law, gravity's weakness, and the reason the geometrogenesis experiments could not work — all fall out of "a bound loop sheds exhaust that pushes-and-feeds." Most parts pre-exist; the assembly and the retro-diagnosis are new.

## 1. The starting point: the freeze problem and the slow clock

RAW 134 Addendum F closed Exp 138 with a negative: directed forward growth (the framework's arrow) produced no geometric phase — only explosion, freeze, or fragmentation. The worry Tom raised: **the experiments ran the gravity-analog at c.** Growth is c (the frontier, causality, the sample-rate limit — RAW 134's founding axiom). But we also fired selection/decay at full strength *every tick* — i.e. we made gravity a c-rate operation. Real gravity is not: it is a whisper (coupling ~10⁻³⁹) that becomes visible only after enormous numbers of light-crossing times. Collapse that hierarchy to 1:1 and you get freeze (selection wins instantly) or blow-up (growth wins) — never the slow accretion between, *because the between is the timescale separation itself.*

Tom's resolution flipped the sign of the worry: **the fast substrate is not the enemy — it is what gives a slow pattern room to breathe.** Because expansion runs at c, a pattern gets many substrate-ticks to complete one of its own re-renderings. Its clock is set by its extent — how long its internal loop takes to come back around — not by c. A big pattern re-renders slowly against a fast background; the gravitational update happens on the *pattern's* clock, which is intrinsically sub-c. The experiments froze because their "patterns" were one tick old — born and judged in the same step — so their clock *equalled* c. No slack, no proper time, instant lock.

This is not a new mechanism to invent. It is the **tick-budget γ earned in Exp 128 v11**: a pattern's clock runs slower the longer its internal path. "The pattern re-renders in its own clock speed" *is* proper time. Gravity being sub-c falls straight out of it. (Earned.)

And the other half of Tom's image — **"the time deposits are still embedded in the final visible 3D structure"** — is the fossil record: the pattern re-renders by reading the deposits its previous renderings laid down, and the visible 3D structure *is* that trail. Space is the pattern's rendering of itself, crystallized. This is Doc 49 ("space from time-differences") and RAW 134 §13.2b (object-is-an-observer) restated: the past does not re-thermalize (the immutable-past axiom forbids it); it grows, and the pattern re-projects itself into the fresh substrate each proper-tick.

## 2. Holding necessarily sheds (and why at c)

The question this raised: last, we had split the problem into "how does a pattern hold" (call it solved-enough — the self-maintenance line of Exp 136/138, cycle closure, Doc 28 renewal) and "how does one pattern reach another." Tom's next move fused them: **a self-closing loop necessarily sheds what it cannot reincorporate.**

This is **Prigogine's dissipative-structures theorem** (Nobel 1977): a structure maintained far from equilibrium *must* export entropy to persist — "entropy is the price of structure" (openness + far-from-equilibrium + nonlinearity + **dissipation** are the four required conditions). A pattern that holds by re-rendering, each cycle, has deposits it can't fold back into the active loop; since the past is frozen and the frontier moves at c, they are carried off. **Holding ⇒ shedding.** The coupling channel is therefore not a second mechanism to build — it is the *guaranteed exhaust* of the first. (Earned in the general thermodynamic literature; newly *identified* with the framework's shed-deposit stream here.)

Why at c specifically — a framework-native answer: a deposit *in* the loop is bound to the pattern's slow clock; a deposit thrown *out* has no loop, no clock of its own, so it reverts to the bare frontier rate, c. **Bound = slow, unbound = c.** This is the cleanest account the project has of why light is fast and matter is slow: they are the same deposits, one still in an engine, one thrown clear of it. (Assembled.)

## 4. Push and pull: opposite signs from one stream

The hard fork — does the coupling attract (gravity) or repel (radiation pressure)? Tom's answer: **both**, and they point opposite ways, which is the engine, not a fudge.

- **Push (repulsive):** absorbed radiation carries momentum; it shoves the receiver away from the source. Radiation pressure.
- **Consume-and-grow (attractive):** the receiver consumes the arriving deposits and grows on the side they arrive from — its body drifts *up-flux*, toward the source. This is precisely RAW 130's consumption-as-centripetal ("it rotates because it consumes," F = −consumed/r²) and RAW 133's leak-recruitment (a neighbor's deposits bias your re-render toward it). (Earned internally.)

The attraction goes as consumed flux, and the flux **dilutes as 1/r²** because the shed deposits spread over the expanding frontier, whose shell count grows as N(r) ∝ r² — *the same exponent that makes rendered space 3D* (RAW 134 Addendum E: Newton's tail and 3D-ness are one exponent). So **attraction ∝ 1/r², Newton**, with the exponent sourced from the area law rather than assumed. (The 1/r²-from-N(r)∝r² is earned in Addendum E; its appearance *here as the radiation dilution* is the new tie.)

## 5. The crossover is the Eddington limit

Which effect wins is set by how much the receiver can consume:

- **Below capacity:** consumption dominates → net attraction (gravity), ∝ 1/r².
- **At/above capacity:** the excess flux it *cannot* consume just pushes → net repulsion (radiation pressure).

The balance point — outward radiation pressure (∝ luminosity/flux) equal to inward consumption-pull (∝ consumed flux, tracking enclosed mass) — is the **Eddington limit**, and in mainstream astrophysics it is a *self-regulator*: the same photon-momentum mechanism that pulls (via the mass it traces) pushes (via the flux), and their equality caps accretion luminosity (Eddington-limited accretion onto stars and black holes). RAW 118 already named "gravity and radiation-pressure as two regimes of one saturation curve, Eddington = receptor saturation threshold" — this document supplies the mechanism under that assertion: the two regimes are consume-wins vs push-wins on one shed stream. (The Eddington balance and its self-regulation are earned/mainstream; the mechanism-under-RAW-118 is the contribution.)

A bonus this hands us: **why gravity is weak.** Only the small fraction of the shed flux that gets *re-consumed as binding* pulls; the rest streams away as light. The gravitational coupling is the tiny absorption-into-binding cross-section of a mostly-escaping radiative flux. A candidate account of the 10⁻³⁹, banked not claimed. (Assembled, speculative.)

## 6. The timescale lock: attraction beats the push only for a slow pattern

The push and the pull are not simply opposed vectors — they act at different *rates*. The push is a fast, c-channel kick: it arrives every substrate-tick and, against a slowly-re-rendering body, **averages to jitter**. The consumption-growth is slow and **directional**: it accumulates as a steady drift over many pattern-cycles. So the attraction survives the competition *iff* the pattern's clock is slow enough for the fast push to wash out while the slow drift accumulates.

This locks §1 and §4 into one requirement: **the sub-c pattern clock is exactly what separates the repulsive push (fast, averages to noise) from the attractive growth (slow, accumulates).** With no timescale separation — the frozen one-tick "patterns" of Exp 138 — push and pull are both per-tick, never separate, and you get the shred-or-freeze mush. The proper-clock reframe and the radiation-coupling are the same condition seen twice. (New synthesis.)

## 7. Runaway → cap → the comoving phase (retro-diagnosis of Exp 138)

Consume-makes-you-grow-makes-you-consume-more is **positive feedback**, and positive feedback is *literally the Exp 138 P1b explosion* (supercritical growth, no stable size). What caps it? The push. At saturation the excess flux shoves back and throttles growth. So push and pull *together* self-regulate at the Eddington balance — and a growth rate pinned at that balance is precisely the **comoving, self-critical, polynomial-in-time phase** RAW 134 Addendum F §18.2 specified as the true target ("the geometric phase grows, hidden by scale; it is not absolute stationarity").

Therefore the sharpest consequence of the whole thread: **Exp 138 could not have found the geometric phase, because its substrate had no exhaust at all.** The selector was pure removal — decay with no shed-and-reconsume channel — so there was nothing to cap the runaway and nothing to feed directional growth. No radiation, no regulator, no comoving phase; only explosion (growth wins) or freeze (decay wins). This is a *diagnosis with a fix*, not a consolation: the missing ingredient is a concrete, buildable thing — the shed → dilute-as-1/r² → reconsume loop — and it predicts the geometric phase should appear at the push-pull balance once that loop exists. (New; this is the document's operational payoff.)

## 9. Falsifiable claims / banked experiment

The claim is not "gravity is tunable" but structural:

1. **Minimum extent.** There is a pattern extent below which everything freezes (the Exp 138 regime) and above which gravity-rate falls as ~1/extent (the proper clock). Below the threshold, no attraction; above it, weak-and-slow attraction.
2. **The exhaust experiment (the real P1d).** Give the substrate a shed → 1/r²-dilute → reconsume channel with momentum (push) *and* consumption-growth (pull) on the same stream, patterns with genuine extent (a slow clock, not one-tick births), and sweep the flux/capacity ratio. Pre-registered outcomes: **(a)** at low ratio, attraction with a growth rate self-pinned near the Eddington balance → comoving polynomial-in-time growth with stationary shape (the geometric phase); **(b)** at high ratio, radiation-pressure blow-apart; **(c)** if push wins at *all* ratios → the mechanism is refuted (sign-competition fails), a clean negative. Reachable-range rule (per the Exp 137/138 lessons): confirm both PASS and FAIL cells are attainable before freezing; and — the Exp 138 P1c lesson — **validate the geometry instrument against a random-graph null first**, or no PASS is certifiable.
3. **Eddington ratio as an order parameter.** If the framework is right, the sub/super-critical boundary of structure formation should track the push-pull (Eddington) ratio, not a bare growth rate — a scan should show the comoving band centered on the balance.
