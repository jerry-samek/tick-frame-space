# Tick‑Frame Geodesic Drive

**A Physically Realistic High‑Velocity Propulsion Mechanism in Tick‑Frame Space**

**Document ID:** 058
**Author:** Tom (Tick‑Frame Research Library)  
**Status:** Draft 1  
**Scope:** Defines the geodesic‑driven propulsion mechanism that enables complex patterns (e.g., rockets, humans) to
achieve high effective velocities (0.9c and beyond) without violating substrate causal limits.

---

## 1. Motivation

In tick‑frame physics:

- **Active propulsion** is fundamentally limited by pattern complexity.
- Complex entities (rockets, humans) must spend most of their tick‑budget on **internal regeneration**, leaving little
  budget for **active motion**.
- This imposes a strict upper bound on active velocity, far below the substrate speed \(c_{\text{sub}}\).

Therefore:

> **No complex object can actively accelerate itself to relativistic speeds.**

Yet relativistic motion clearly exists in nature.  
The solution is **geodesic motion**, not active thrust.

---

## 2. Core Principle

A **geodesic** in tick‑frame space is the path that **maximizes proper time per tick** for a pattern embedded in a
dynamic load–energy field.

Patterns do not “push” themselves through the substrate.  
Instead:

> **The field carries the pattern.**

This bypasses the active propulsion limit entirely.

---

## 3. Field Dynamics (Summary)

Tick‑frame gravity emerges from two coupled fields:

1. **Load field \(L(x,t)\)**
    - Reaction–diffusion dynamics
    - Represents computational demand

2. **Energy field \(E(x,t)\)**
    - Regenerative capacity
    - Represents available tick‑budget

The **time‑flow field** is:

\[
\gamma(x,t) = \frac{\text{ticks processed}}{\text{substrate ticks}}
\]

Entities follow the gradient:

\[
\vec{a} = k \cdot \nabla \gamma
\]

This is the tick‑frame analogue of the geodesic equation.

---

## 4. Geodesic Drive: Definition

**The Tick‑Frame Geodesic Drive** is a propulsion mechanism where:

- The entity does **not** expend tick‑budget on motion.
- The entity’s position changes because the **field configuration evolves**.
- The entity simply follows the **path of maximal proper time**.

Formally:

\[
\frac{d x}{d t} = f(\nabla \gamma)
\]

with no dependence on internal complexity.

---

## 5. Why Geodesic Drive Allows 0.9c

### 5.1 Active propulsion limit (for comparison)

For a complex pattern:

\[
v_{\text{active}} \leq c_{\text{sub}} \cdot (1 - C_{\text{int}})
\]

where \(C_{\text{int}}\) is internal complexity cost.

For humans, rockets, or any macroscopic object:

\[
C_{\text{int}} \approx 1 \quad \Rightarrow \quad v_{\text{active}} \approx 0
\]

### 5.2 Geodesic drive bypasses this entirely

Because:

- The pattern does **not** perform the motion.
- The **field** performs the motion.
- Internal complexity only affects regeneration, not displacement.

Thus:

\[
v_{\text{eff}}^{\text{geodesic}} \text{ can approach } c_{\text{sub}}
\]

even for extremely complex patterns.

This is the tick‑frame analogue of:

- gravitational slingshots,
- free fall,
- frame dragging,
- cosmological expansion.

- A rocket cannot accelerate itself to 0.9c.
- But a rocket can **enter a geodesic** that yields 0.9c relative to another frame.
- This is the only physically consistent high‑velocity propulsion mechanism in tick‑frame physics.

Examples:

- gravitational slingshot around a massive field,
- engineered γ‑gradients,
- dynamic field shaping (future tech).
