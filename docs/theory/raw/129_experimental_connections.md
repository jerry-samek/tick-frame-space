## 1. Breit-Wheeler Process: Photons → Matter (E = mc²)

### The experiment
Brookhaven STAR experiment (2021): two real photons collide, produce an
electron-positron pair. Pure energy → matter. Published in Physical Review
Letters. Over 6,000 pairs observed.

First predicted by Breit & Wheeler in 1934. First observed with virtual
photons at SLAC in 1997. Confirmed with real photons at Brookhaven in 2021.

### Tick-frame interpretation
Two propagating deposit patterns (photons = all-move in the RAW 128
partition) collide at a graph node. The combined deposit flux exceeds the
threshold for a new entity to crystallize. The entity (electron-positron
pair) IS the deposits, restructured from "propagating pattern" (all-move)
into "stationary pattern" (all-store).

- **Photon** = deposit pattern propagating at c, never absorbed (all-move)
- **Matter** = deposit pattern stored locally, not propagating (all-store)
- **E = mc²** = the partition flipping from move to store
- **Pair production** = the deposits reorganize from one pattern (photon)
  into two patterns (electron + positron) when the local deposit density
  exceeds the crystallization threshold

### The real/virtual distinction
The Brookhaven experiment had to verify the photons were "real" (propagated
through space) vs "virtual" (short-range field fluctuations). The angular
distribution of produced pairs differs for real vs virtual photon interactions.

In the tick-frame model:
- **Real photon** = deposits propagated along actual connectors (long path,
  many hops, well-defined direction)
- **Virtual photon** = deposits on very short connectors (near-field, minimal
  propagation, no well-defined direction)

The distinction exists naturally in the theory: real = propagated, virtual =
local. The angular distribution difference follows from the directionality
of the deposits.

  Matter/Antimatter from Pure Energy"

## 5. Quantum Battery Superabsorption: Bigger = Faster Charging

### The experiment
Quantum batteries charge FASTER as they get LARGER. Demonstrated in organic
microcavity systems (Science Advances, 2022) and superconducting quantum
processors (2024-2025). The charging power scales SUPERLINEARLY with the
number of molecules — double the size charges in LESS than half the time.

The mechanism: **superabsorption.** Molecules strongly coupled inside a
cavity don't absorb energy individually. They absorb COLLECTIVELY as a
single quantum event. The collective absorption rate scales faster than N.

First prototype quantum battery demonstrated at RMIT University (March 2026):
holds energy 1000x longer than prior models. Light-to-charge conversion
power scales super-linearly with system size even under low-intensity,
incoherent illumination.

### Tick-frame interpretation
This maps directly to entity boundary consumption. More surface nodes on
an entity = more collective processing capacity. But not linearly — the
nodes interact with each other through shared Same deposits, creating
collective behavior.

- **Individual absorption** (N=1): single node processes 1 deposit per tick.
  Rate = 1.
- **Collective absorption** (N=many): surface nodes share Same deposits,
  creating coupled capacitors. When one fires, it influences neighbors.
  The collective rate > N × individual rate.
- **Superlinear scaling**: the Same-routing between surface nodes creates
  a feedback loop. One node's consumption triggers rebalancing of deposits
  on shared connectors, which charges neighboring nodes' capacitors faster.

In our Experiment 128 v3 data: 5 planet nodes process deposits individually
(no collective behavior — they're too spread out to couple). At N=500+,
planet nodes would be close enough that their shared Same deposits create
the coupling. The collective absorption should emerge naturally.

### The "bigger = faster" prediction

- 5 nodes: rate ~ 5 (individual)
- 50 nodes: rate >> 50 (collective coupling begins)
- 500 nodes: rate >>> 500 (fully collective)

This is testable in the simulation: create planets of different sizes

### The connection to quantum-classical transition
Superabsorption IS the mechanism that bridges quantum and classical:
- Small entities (few nodes): individual absorption, quantum noise dominates
- Large entities (many nodes): collective absorption, classical behavior emerges
- The transition scale is where collective coupling exceeds thermal noise

This means the quantum-classical boundary is not about "measurement" or
"decoherence." It's about entity SIZE. Larger entities absorb collectively,
which produces deterministic (classical) behavior. Smaller entities absorb
individually, which produces probabilistic (quantum) behavior.

root consumes the most common patterns, children process the rejects, depth
