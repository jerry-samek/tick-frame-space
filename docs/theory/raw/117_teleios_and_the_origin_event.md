## 1. Teleios — What Standard Physics Must Explain

Teleios (G305.4–2.2) was serendipitously discovered in ASKAP radio-continuum survey data
and reported in May 2025 (Filipović et al., 2025). Its properties:

- **Near-perfect circular symmetry** — among the most circular Galactic SNRs known
- **Exclusively radio** — no X-ray, no significant optical, only radio-continuum emission
- **Lowest surface brightness** among known Galactic SNRs
- **Steep spectral index** α = −0.6 ± 0.3
- **Rotation measure highest at center** — not at the shell where swept-up material is
- **Distance uncertain** — either ~2.2 kpc or ~7.7 kpc, implying diameter 14–48 pc
- **No definitive origin type** — all examined scenarios have challenges

The authors note: "Teleios's symmetrical shape suggests expansion into a rarefied and
isotropic ambient medium." This description maps directly to the tick-frame substrate.
