## Abstract

The tick-frame model produces gravitational binding and radial oscillation
(Experiment 118 v7), but orbital motion requires a mechanism for tangential
velocity — a directional memory that carries an entity past the gravitational
center rather than letting it reverse at perihelion. This document identifies
that mechanism as an energy partition at the quantum level: each deposit
quantum discharged by an entity's capacitor has one of **three outcomes** —
**stored** (absorbed by the next node's idle capacitor, becoming mass),
**continues as motion** (the next node's capacitor is busy, quantum
propagates forward), or **radiated** (the next node's capacitor is busy,
quantum propagates sideways or outward, leaving the system).

**Critical:** This is not a choice. There is no agent deciding the outcome.
There is only: a capacitor discharges, the quantum arrives at the next node,
and that node's capacitor is either idle (absorbs → store) or busy (can't
absorb → quantum continues in whatever direction the graph provides). Whether
the continuation is forward (momentum) or sideways/outward (radiation)
depends on the graph topology at that node. The local environment determines
everything.

The three-way partition unifies mass, momentum, kinetic energy, potential
energy, radiation, thermal equilibrium, and orbital stability within the
single-mechanism framework.

- **E = mc²** emerges as the all-store limit
- **The photon** emerges as all-move (a discharge that never finds an idle capacitor)
- **Planetary radiation** (thermal emission) emerges as the sideways/outward fraction
- **Orbital stability** emerges because radiation provides an energy OUTLET — without
  it, the planet accumulates energy forever and the orbit spirals outward

## 1. The Problem: Why Radial Oscillation Is Not an Orbit

Experiment 118 v7 demonstrated gravitational binding: a planet routes toward
a star's deposit field, approaches, overshoots, recedes, approaches again.
Radial oscillation. A bound state.

But without seeded tangential velocity, the oscillation is purely radial —
the planet bounces along a line through the star. Zero angular momentum.
When tangential velocity was manually seeded (Phase 2b), the planet produced
sustained orbital motion (3.9 net revolutions over 200k ticks). The mechanism
preserves and even amplifies angular momentum.

The missing piece: **what generates tangential velocity from the mechanism
itself?** In the v7 model, each entity node makes a fresh routing decision
at each arrival based on local deposit counts. There is no memory of which
direction the node was traveling. No inertia. No momentum. The node reads
the field and routes — every hop is independent.

In real physics, a falling body gains momentum in the direction of the
gravitational force. That momentum persists (Newton's first law) even
after the force direction changes. The body swings past perihelion rather
than reversing. The orbit exists because momentum is conserved while the
force direction rotates.

The tick-frame model needs an equivalent of momentum. Not as an added
parameter — as a consequence of the single mechanism.

## 2. One Quantum, Three Outcomes

### 2.1 The Discharge Event

An entity's capacitor fires. One quantum is discharged onto an outgoing
connector. The quantum travels along the connector toward the next node.
This is the single mechanism: discharge → deposit → propagate.

The entity doesn't choose what happens next. The quantum is in transit.

### 2.2 Arrival at the Next Node

The quantum arrives at the node at the other end of the connector. That
node has a capacitor (or doesn't — unclaimed space). Three outcomes:

**STORED**: The receiving node's capacitor is idle (not currently
processing another deposit). The capacitor absorbs the quantum. The
quantum becomes part of the local structure. The connector between
the two nodes now has one more deposit in its history. Mass increases.

**CONTINUES FORWARD (MOMENTUM)**: The receiving node's capacitor is
busy (or absent). The quantum cannot be absorbed. It continues along
the connector most aligned with its arrival direction (forward
continuation on the graph). This quantum is now part of the entity's
**momentum wake** — propagating ahead of the entity, biasing future
routing decisions forward.

**CONTINUES SIDEWAYS/OUTWARD (RADIATION)**: The receiving node's
capacitor is busy (or absent), AND the forward direction is also
blocked (that connector is saturated or leads to another busy node).
The quantum takes a DIFFERENT outgoing connector — one that leads
sideways or away from the entity's travel direction. This quantum
leaves the entity's neighborhood. It is **radiated** — energy that
exits the system.

### 2.3 There Is No Choice

None of the three outcomes is chosen by the entity. The receiving
environment determines everything:

1. Capacitor fires → quantum goes to next node
2. Next node idle → absorbed → **stored**
3. Next node busy, forward path available → **momentum**
4. Next node busy, forward path blocked → **radiated**

The entity that discharged has no say in the outcome. The RECEIVING
node's capacitor state and the LOCAL graph topology determine whether
the quantum becomes mass, momentum, or radiation.

### 2.4 What Determines the Ratios?

The three-way partition is determined entirely by **capacitor availability
and graph topology** in the local environment:

| Regime | Local environment | Store | Move | Radiate |
|--------|------------------|-------|------|---------|
| **All neighbors idle** | Sparse, low traffic | High | Low | Low |
| **Mixed availability** | Boundary, moderate | Mixed | Mixed | Mixed |
| **All neighbors busy** | Dense, high traffic | Zero | Some | Some |

In the all-busy regime, the split between MOVE and RADIATE depends on
the graph geometry: how many forward-aligned connectors exist vs how
many lead sideways. On a random geometric graph with k≈24, roughly
k/4 ≈ 6 connectors face any given direction. The rest face other
directions. So the move/radiate split is approximately 1:3 in the
all-busy regime — for every quantum that becomes forward momentum,
three are radiated sideways.

## 3. Momentum Without a Momentum Variable

### 3.1 Motion IS Propagation

When a quantum can't be absorbed at the next node, it continues along
the graph. It propagates from node to node, attempting absorption at
each one, continuing whenever the local capacitor is busy or absent.

The quantum's direction is determined by the graph topology — it
follows the forward-continuation path (the connector most aligned with
its arrival direction, from the graph embedding geometry). It doesn't
need a velocity vector. It's just a deposit that hasn't been absorbed
yet, propagating through the graph at c = 1 hop/tick.

### 3.2 Momentum is Accumulated Unabsorbed Discharges

An entity's "momentum" is the collection of its unabsorbed discharge
quanta that are currently propagating through the graph ahead of it.
These quanta carry the entity's pattern in the entity's recent travel
direction. When the entity arrives at a new node, these quanta have
already deposited on the forward connectors — making the forward
direction look richer in matching deposits. The entity's next routing
decision is therefore biased forward.

**Momentum is not stored in the entity. It is stored in the graph
ahead of the entity** — as deposits that were discharged but not
absorbed, propagating forward, creating a wake of matching deposits
in the entity's travel direction.

### 3.3 Perihelion Swing-Through

As the planet approaches the star, the star's interior capacitors are
all busy (processing internal traffic). The planet's discharges toward
the star **can't be absorbed** — they propagate straight through the
star and out the other side. These discharges deposit on connectors
beyond the star, in the planet's forward direction.

When the planet reaches perihelion and its routing says "turn back
toward the star" (deposit count is highest there), the forward
deposits — the planet's own unabsorbed discharges — compete with
the star's field. The forward direction has the planet's own matching
deposits. The routing is biased forward. The planet swings through
rather than reversing.

This is inertia. Not as a law — as a consequence of busy capacitors
at the star preventing absorption of the planet's discharges.

### 3.4 Aphelion Deceleration

Far from the star, the planet's discharges encounter idle capacitors
(sparse space). Every discharge is absorbed immediately. No forward
deposits accumulate. No momentum wake. The planet's routing is
dominated by the gravitational signal (star deposits behind it).
It slows and falls back.

### 3.5 No Momentum Parameter

There is no momentum parameter, no forward-bias weight, no inertia
constant. The momentum mechanism is entirely determined by:
- Whether the planet's discharges get absorbed or propagate
- Which depends on local capacitor availability
- Which depends on the density of entities near the planet's path

Heavy traffic region → discharges propagate → momentum builds.
Empty region → discharges absorbed → momentum dissipates.

This IS friction. A body moving through a dense medium loses momentum
because the medium's capacitors absorb its discharges. A body moving
through vacuum maintains momentum because there's nothing to absorb
its discharges. Both follow from the same mechanism.

## 4. Radiation: The Energy Outlet

### 4.1 Why Orbits Need an Energy Outlet

Without radiation, the planet only accumulates energy. Star deposits
arrive (energy in). Some become mass (stored). Some become momentum
(motion). But neither leaves the system. Over time:

- Mass grows → connectors grow → local space expands → orbit spirals out
- Momentum grows → velocity increases → orbit becomes eccentric

There is no equilibrium because there is no energy OUTLET. The planet
heats up forever.

**Radiation is the outlet.** Quanta that go sideways or outward — not
forward (momentum) and not absorbed (mass) — leave the planet's
neighborhood and propagate into the wider graph. They are gone. The
planet has shed energy.

### 4.2 Thermal Equilibrium on the Graph

At orbital equilibrium, the planet's energy budget balances:

```
energy in (star deposits arriving) =
    energy stored (mass growth) +
    energy moved (momentum maintenance) +
    energy radiated (quanta leaving sideways/outward)
```

For a **stable circular orbit**: energy stored ≈ 0 (no net mass growth),
energy moved ≈ 0 (no net acceleration). Therefore:

```
energy in ≈ energy radiated
```

The planet absorbs from the star and radiates back into space. The
balance determines the "temperature" — the rate at which the planet
re-emits energy. This IS planetary thermal equilibrium.

### 4.3 Negative Feedback → Stability

If the planet absorbs MORE than it radiates:
- Excess goes to mass (connectors grow) or momentum (speeds up)
- If momentum: planet moves to higher orbit → less flux → less
  absorption → balance restored
- If mass: local space expands → effective distance increases →
  less flux → balance restored

If the planet radiates MORE than it absorbs:
- Deficit comes from mass (connectors shrink? no — append-only) or
  momentum (slows down)
- Planet slows → falls to lower orbit → more flux → more absorption
  → balance restored

**Both directions lead back to equilibrium.** This is negative feedback.
The orbit is stable because radiation provides a release valve that
self-corrects the energy budget.

**Append-only constraint:** Connectors can't shrink (no deletion). So
the "mass loss" pathway doesn't exist. The only adjustment is velocity
(momentum). A planet that radiates too much slows down and falls
inward. A planet that absorbs too much speeds up and climbs outward.
The orbit self-corrects through velocity, not mass.

### 4.4 Mapping to Real Planetary Physics

Earth absorbs ~240 W/m² from the Sun. Earth radiates ~240 W/m² as
thermal (infrared) emission. The balance determines Earth's surface
temperature (~288 K).

In the graph model: the planet's entity nodes absorb star deposits
(energy in) and re-emit quanta sideways/outward (energy out). The
rate of sideways emission depends on local capacitor busyness and
graph topology. At equilibrium, the absorption rate equals the
radiation rate. The "temperature" is the radiation rate.

A planet closer to the star absorbs more → radiates more → higher
"temperature." A planet farther from the star absorbs less → radiates
less → lower "temperature." Mercury is hot. Neptune is cold. Same
mechanism, different flux.

### 4.5 What Determines the Radiation Direction?

The radiated quantum goes onto whichever outgoing connector the graph
provides that isn't forward. On a random geometric graph with k≈24
neighbors, about 6 face roughly forward, 6 face roughly backward
(toward the star), and 12 face roughly sideways. The sideways
connectors are the radiation channels.

The radiation is approximately isotropic in the plane perpendicular
to the orbit. It is NOT preferentially directed away from the star.
The planet radiates in all non-forward, non-starward directions
equally. This matches real thermal emission — planets radiate
approximately isotropically, not directionally.

## 5. E = mc² as the Storage Limit

### 5.1 All Stored

If an entity's every discharge is immediately absorbed by neighboring
idle capacitors, nothing propagates. Nothing radiates. The entity
deposits locally, grows in mass, slows its clock. Maximum mass, zero
velocity, zero radiation. In natural units (c = 1 hop/tick):
E_stored = m. This is E = mc² with c = 1.

### 5.2 All Motion

If an entity's every discharge propagates forward (nothing absorbed,
nothing radiated sideways), the entity contributes zero mass to its
local structure. It moves at c = 1 hop/tick. It's a photon — a
discharge pattern that never found an idle capacitor and never got
deflected sideways.

### 5.3 All Radiated

If an entity's every discharge goes sideways (nothing stored, nothing
forward), the entity neither grows nor moves. It sits in place,
converting incoming energy to outward radiation. It's a **blackbody**
— a perfect absorber-emitter. Maximum radiation, zero mass growth,
zero velocity.

### 5.4 The Three-Way Spectrum

| All stored | Mixed | All motion | All radiated |
|-----------|-------|-----------|-------------|
| Black hole | Orbiting matter | Photon | Blackbody |
| v = 0, max mass | 0 < v < c | v = c, zero mass | v = 0, zero mass |
| Clock stopped | Clock running | No proper time | Clock running, static |

Every entity occupies a position in this three-dimensional partition
space. The position is not intrinsic — it depends on the local
capacitor environment and graph topology. The same entity has different
store/move/radiate ratios at different locations in the graph.

## 6. Orbital Mechanics from the Three-Way Partition

### 6.1 The Complete Orbit

```
Aphelion (sparse → all stored → no momentum → no radiation → falls inward)
    ↓
Falling (increasing density → more motion → accelerating → some radiation)
    ↓
Perihelion (star core → all busy → max momentum + max radiation)
    ↓
Swing-through (momentum wake carries past → radiation bleeds energy)
    ↓
Climbing (decreasing density → more stored → decelerating → less radiation)
    ↓
Aphelion (sparse again → cycle repeats)
```

The orbit exists because the three-way ratio CHANGES with position.
Radiation provides the energy outlet that prevents indefinite energy
accumulation. The orbit is stable because radiation self-corrects the
energy budget.

### 6.2 Eccentricity from Capacity Mismatch

A small planet (few nodes) has few capacitors to discharge. Near the
star (busy environment), all quanta propagate or radiate. Far from the
star, all quanta get absorbed. Sharp transition = high eccentricity.

A large planet (many nodes) has many capacitors. The collective
discharge overwhelms the local environment even at moderate distances.
Gradual transition = low eccentricity.

### 6.3 Kepler's Laws

**First law (elliptical orbits):** The orbit is determined by the
graph topology and the radial profile of capacitor availability. If
the availability profile is smooth and approximately spherical, the
orbit should be approximately elliptical. Graph irregularities produce
deviations (precession).

**Second law (equal areas):** At perihelion, the planet moves fast
(max momentum). At aphelion, it moves slow (all stored). The product
r × v should be approximately constant if the mechanism conserves
angular momentum. Testable in v9.

**Third law (T² ∝ r³):** The orbital period depends on the circumference
and velocity profile around the orbit. Whether this produces T² ∝ r³
is a quantitative test for v9.

## 7. What This Opens

### 7.1 Resolved

**The store/move decision rule**: there IS no decision. The receiving
capacitor's availability determines the outcome. Idle → stored.
Busy → continues. The direction of continuation (forward vs sideways)
is determined by graph topology. No rule needed.

**The momentum formula**: momentum = unabsorbed discharges propagating
ahead of the entity. Emergent from capacitor availability.

**Orbital stability**: radiation provides the energy outlet. Without
it, orbits spiral. With it, the energy budget self-corrects.

### 7.2 Still Open

1. **The stream origin** — unchanged (RAW 123 §4, RAW 126 §4)
2. **Multi-node aggregate behavior** — does the per-node mechanism
   produce classical orbital mechanics when aggregated?
3. **Quantitative tests** — Kepler's laws, virial theorem, 1/r² scaling
4. **Radiation spectrum** — does the distribution of radiated quanta
   match a thermal spectrum? Does the "temperature" scale with
   distance from the star as 1/r^(1/2) (Stefan-Boltzmann)?

### 7.3 The v9 Experiment

Implement:
1. Discharge → propagate to next node → check capacitor availability
   → absorbed (store) or continue
2. If continuing: forward-aligned → momentum; sideways/outward → radiation
3. Track the three-way partition ratio at the planet as function of distance
4. Measure energy budget: star flux in, planet radiation out
5. Test: does the partition ratio change with distance as predicted?
6. Test: does the orbit stabilize (constant mean radius over many periods)?

## 8. Summary

One quantum is discharged. It arrives at the next node.

| Next node idle | Next node busy (forward) | Next node busy (sideways) |
|---------------|-------------------------|--------------------------|
| Absorbed | Continues forward | Continues outward |
| **Stored as mass** | **Momentum wake** | **Radiated away** |
| Connector grows | Entity accelerates | Energy leaves system |
| Clock slows | Inertia builds | Temperature maintained |

**There is no choice. There is no agent. There is only local capacitor
availability and graph topology.**

The orbit exists because:
- Near the star → all capacitors busy → momentum + radiation
- Far from star → all capacitors idle → mass (stored)
- The transition produces the swing-through at perihelion
- Radiation bleeds excess energy → orbit doesn't spiral → stability

The complete energy partition:

```
Energy in (star deposits) = Store (mass) + Move (momentum) + Radiate (light/heat)
```

At orbital equilibrium: Store ≈ 0, Move ≈ 0, therefore:
**Energy in ≈ Energy radiated.** The orbit is a self-correcting
energy balance maintained by radiation.

The photon is all-move (never absorbed, never deflected).
Mass is all-store (immediately absorbed).
The planet is the balance of all three.

**One quantum. Three outcomes. No choice. The environment decides.**
