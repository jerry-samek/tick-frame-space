# **A Foundational Theory of Magnetism in the Tick‑Frame Universe**
### *Pattern Dynamics, Gamma Geometry, and the Emergence of Magnetic Phenomena*

---

## **Abstract**

This paper presents a unified theory of magnetism within the tick‑frame universe, a computational ontology in which all physical phenomena emerge from the interaction between discrete *patterns* and the *gamma field*, the local rate of temporal progression. Unlike classical physics, which treats electromagnetism as a fundamental force, the tick‑frame model derives magnetic behavior from anisotropies in the gamma field generated by moving or rotating patterns. This framework reproduces classical magnetic effects—currents, dipoles, ferromagnetism, and macroscopic magnetic fields—without invoking Maxwell’s equations as primitives. Instead, magnetism is shown to be a geometric property of time itself.

## **1. Introduction**

In conventional physics, magnetism is described through Maxwell’s equations and the Lorentz force. These equations treat electric and magnetic fields as fundamental entities, with no deeper substrate.

The tick‑frame universe proposes a different ontology:

- **Patterns** are discrete, self‑maintaining computational structures.
- **Gamma** is the effective local rate of time flow, derived from capacity\(_\text{eff}\).
- **Motion** is geodesic evolution through gamma geometry.

Within this framework, classical forces are not fundamental. Instead:

- Gravity emerges from static gamma wells.
- Electric fields emerge from gamma gradients.
- Magnetic fields emerge from gamma anisotropies caused by moving or rotating patterns.

This paper formalizes the tick‑frame theory of magnetism and demonstrates how classical magnetic phenomena arise naturally from temporal geometry.

- electrons, protons, atoms

- macroscopic objects

- **load** (L)

Gamma is defined as:

\[
\gamma = \frac{1}{\text{capacity}_\text{eff}}

It represents the *local speed of time*.

- High gamma → time flows quickly
- Low gamma → time flows slowly
- Gradients in gamma → directional drift of patterns
- Anisotropies in gamma → lateral drift (magnetic effects)

Gamma is the only continuous field in the ontology.

### **2.3 Motion as Geodesics**

Patterns move along geodesics defined by gamma:

- Toward regions of faster time
- Away from regions of slower time
- Sideways when gamma is directionally skewed

This replaces the classical concept of “forces.”

## **3. Magnetism as Gamma Anisotropy**

- No preferred direction
- No magnetic effects

### **3.2 Moving Patterns Produce Forward Gamma Deformation**

When a pattern moves:

> **A rotating pattern twists the gamma field into a spiral.  
> This spiral anisotropy is magnetism.**

This produces:

- lateral drift of nearby patterns
- circular or helical trajectories
- magnetic dipole behavior

No magnetic “force” is invoked—only geometry.
