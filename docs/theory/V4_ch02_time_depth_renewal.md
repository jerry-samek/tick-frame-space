# Chapter 2: Time, Depth, and Renewal

*Draws on: RAW 134, V3 ch004 (Time and Depth), Doc 28 (Temporal Surfing),
Doc 50/50_01 (the ρ=2.0 dimensional-equivalence rejection).*

---

## 2.1 Time is the order you read your own depth

In the god-view, time is a coordinate — a line the whole universe is laid out
along. Inside-out, there is no such line. What each pattern has is a strictly
ordered sequence of its own causal calls, and **time is the order in which a
pattern reads its own causal depth.**

Depth is how many recursive calls deep a state is from the root. A pattern advances
in time by making the next call — computing the next tick of itself. It cannot read
a call that has not been made yet. That is the whole content of "the future is
unseeable": the future is not hidden, it is *not yet computed*. There is nothing
there to see because nothing has run.

This dissolves several puzzles at once:

- **Why time has a direction.** Depth only increases (append-only, Chapter 1).
  There is no operation that decreases it. The arrow is the append.
- **Why the present is special.** The present is the frontier of computation — the
  deepest call a pattern has made. Past calls are done; future calls are not run.
- **Why simultaneity is not global.** Two patterns' depths are not on a shared
  ruler. "At the same time" is a relation each observer reconstructs from arrivals
  (Chapter 3), not a fact about the substrate.

## 2.2 Renewal, not persistence (Doc 28)

The single most important dynamical fact — the one that makes the whole gravity
story of Chapter 4 possible — is that **patterns do not persist; they are
renewed.**

An entity is not a lump of stuff that endures from tick to tick. It is a process
that *re-forms itself* at each tick out of the ambient causal flux — the "temporal
surfing" of Doc 28. Each tick, the pattern is re-rendered; if it re-renders in the
same shape, it looks stable, but the substrate underneath it has entirely turned
over. Identity is continuity of *pattern*, not continuity of *material*.

Two consequences carried forward:

1. **A renewing pattern reads at rest in its own frame.** Because it re-forms each
   tick relative to what has arrived, its own bulk velocity is invisible to it —
   it is always "here." This is exactly what defeats the LeSage drag objection in
   Chapter 4: you cannot drag something that re-bases its rest frame every tick.
2. **Renewal is what makes shadow-gravity coherent.** Gravity (Chapter 4) is a
   shadow cast in the *renewal flux*. If patterns persisted instead of renewing,
   there would be no flux to shadow. Renewal is the axiom the engine runs on.

This is also why "empty space is a rendered delay" (Chapter 1) and "time is read
depth" are the same statement seen twice: space between deposits is the delay in
the renewal reaching across, and that delay is measured in depth.

## 2.3 Memory and storage cannot be paused

Because the substrate is append-only and renewing, **nothing can be perfectly
stored.** A memory is a deposit pattern in some medium; that medium is itself part
of the substrate and keeps participating in the dynamics. New arrivals fire Same
(reinforce), Different (modify), or Unknown (extend) at the storage nodes. The
stored state drifts at exactly the local Different-event rate.

There is no way to freeze a region and step outside the flow — stepping outside is
the god-view, which does not exist. A snapshot reloaded after drift instantiates a
*continuation of a modified state*, not the original. This is not corruption; it is
the substrate honestly reporting that it never stopped computing. (Carried from V3
ch004; detailed for information in Chapter 6.)

## 2.4 The ρ=2.0 signature: time is not a spatial dimension

A natural challenge to any "time is emergent" story is: maybe time is just another
spatial dimension. The framework has an experimental answer, and it is a clean
negative for the equivalence (Doc 50 / 50_01, January 2026).

Across 1,095 configurations spanning (2D+t, 3D+t, 4D+t), every (n+t) system
diverges from the pure-spatial (3D, 4D, 5D) baselines, and does so with a specific
signature: **source scaling ρ = 2.0** (quadratic) for every time-including system,
versus sub-quadratic ρ ≈ 1.5 for pure spatial dimensions. Combined with the
kinematic constraints of the earlier experiments (rotation asymmetry, propagation
capped at one tick per tick), this is the framework's evidence that **time is a
special generator** — accumulative and amplifying — and categorically different
from a spatial axis.

Read inside-out, this is exactly what Chapter 1 predicts: time is the *generator*
of the recursion (depth), while space is a *reading* of the resulting delays
(Chapter 3). They cannot scale the same way because they are not the same kind of
thing. The ρ=2.0 result is the fingerprint of that asymmetry.

## 2.5 Status

- **Solid within the ontology:** time-as-read-depth and renewal-not-persistence are
  the core commitments and are internally consistent and load-bearing.
- **Empirically anchored (in-model):** the ρ=2.0 dimensional-equivalence rejection
  is a genuine, reproducible simulation result and is the strongest single piece of
  quantitative support for treating time as a distinct generator.
- **Open:** a fully substrate-native account of the storage-drift *rate* (predicted
  = local Different rate) has not been derived or measured; and renewal is currently
  an axiom (Doc 28), motivated but not itself derived from something more basic.
