**Read this chapter as the truth of the project, not the abstracts of the other
chapters.** This is a speculative model with **no real-world experimental
validation**. What makes it worth taking seriously — if anything does — is the
discipline it is developed under, and the negatives that discipline has produced.

## 8.1 The discipline: the fresh-context skeptic

Every "result" in this project is attacked by a **fresh-context reviewer** — a
skeptic with none of the operator's investment — before it is believed. The single
most productive pattern the project has found is that skeptic pass, and its recurring
verdict, recorded and reused, is a law of this work:

> **When something is stuck, a god-view assumption has been smuggled in.**

Chapters 3–5 are, structurally, that law applied to dimension, gravity, and the
observer. The skeptic pass is also why several headline claims below are *negatives*:
they are the results that survived an adversary trying to kill them, or the claims an
adversary *did* kill.

## 8.2 What has been demonstrated (in-model, mostly toy scale)

- **A candidate gravity mechanism that clears all three classical LeSage problems**
  (Chapter 4), each by the same god-view→inside-out correction: **sign** (ambient
  shadow, not the star's own light; Eddington crossover +0.30 → −0.58), **drag** (a
  renewing pattern reads at rest in its own frame — stable orbit vs a persistent
  body's decay to 0.05·r₀), and **heat** (gravity shadows the renewal *rate*, ~1000×
  cooler; residual heat becomes the coherence limit R_max ∝ √(θ/p)).
- **Dimension is measurable inside-out** (Chapter 3). The boundary-layer,
  lag-correlation reader cleanly separates the exact adversarial zoo (hypercubes,
  honeycomb, expanders, small-world) that defeated every god-view detector — because
  from inside, an expander genuinely *looks* high-dimensional. This is the
  framework's own dimension definition grounded in propagation delay, and it is
  **validated against the nulls that killed the previous instrument.**
- **One substrate does all of it** (Chapter 7): a single `FloodingLagSubstrate`
  yields gravity, perceived dimension, and a coherence limit from one consuming
  pattern, reproducing the standalone gravity toy exactly (+0.2967 at r=2).
- **The relational point of view, concretely** (Chapter 5): "planet orbits star" is
  not privileged; lags are frame-invariant, "who moves" is not.

## 8.3 The rigorous negatives (these are the point)

The negatives are load-bearing, not embarrassing. They are what a self-honest
program looks like.

- **The substrate-side wall — the big one.** Does a growing, directed-time substrate
  actually *select* a low-dimensional (geometric) phase, or a tree/expander? The Exp
  138 P1d **recon leans negative**: the growth engine reads low angular lag-rank
  (1.1–1.3, not expander) — but the decisive tell is that the **`decay=FALSE`
  no-selection control reads *identically* to the selected cell.** So the low-rank
  signal is a topological artifact of multiplicative branching, **not** something the
  selection produces. Directed growth *alone* does not make geometry. The missing
  ingredient the framework identifies — a **dissipation / exhaust channel** driving
  self-organized criticality (RAW 136) — is designed but **not built.** This is the
  geometrogenesis problem, shared with all of discrete quantum gravity, and it is
  **unsolved here.**
- **Exp 138 P1b: a registered negative, corrected by the skeptic.** An "equilibrium
  is load-bearing" claim was **retracted** when the skeptic found the selector
  engaged only **0.022%** of the time and the outcomes were the Euler–Lotka theorem,
  not the proposed mechanism. Lesson banked: *check a selector's engagement before
  interpreting a selection experiment.*
- **Exp 137 K=12 census: a headline withdrawn.** The Exp 134 rule's tie-breaking was
  found **unspecified**; kicks create ties at tick 1, so the readout is not
  representative-invariant and the "box-quadruplet" headline was **withdrawn.** Fix
  tie-breaking before any further instrument work on that rule.
- **Rediscovered textbook results, flagged as such.** Across the 118–133 gravity arc,
  several "emergent" results were caught by code inspection to be **smuggled Newton**
  or rediscovered theorems (Euler–Lotka, additive-growth d=1, the 1/r² ⟺ N(r)∝r²
  identity). Standing rule (`feedback_honest_emergence_claims`): flag Newton/Kepler
  when it is in the code; never claim it as emergent.
- **The god-view instruments failed their own nulls.** The shell-counting / cycle-gate
  dimension detectors were killed this session by a hypercube false-positive (P1c) and
  a honeycomb false-negative — which is *why* Chapter 3's inside-out reader exists.

## 8.4 What has NOT been demonstrated

- **The wall of §8.3** — geometrogenesis — is the gate everything else waits behind.
  Chapters 4, 5, 7 all show physics the substrate can *host once it is geometric*;
  none shows it *becomes* geometric. **3D is not derived** (Chapter 3 §3.6).
- **Toy-scale calibration.** Orbits, wells, and coherence run on the substrate's
  principles but several are ODE / diffusion-level, not fully graph-native; moving
  patterns are ODE-level; decoherence is threshold-proxied.
- **No connection to real-world experimental physics.** The one banked *testable*
  difference from standard QM remains the interferometry prediction (Exp 62:
  which-path detection without destroying interference) — untested.
- **The formal comparison operator** (Same vs Different) is still undefined
  (Chapter 6), which blocks a faithful trie simulation.

## 8.5 The arc in one paragraph

Experiments 118–133 chased gravity on graph substrates and repeatedly produced
*binding without a field, or a field with smuggled Newton* — triangulating the same
gap from many sides (`project_arc_diagnosis_118_135`). Exp 134–137 earned honest,
bit-exact local patterns (transactional renewal on a signed substrate) but **no force
at a distance**, and surfaced the tie-breaking flaw. Exp 138 built the condensation
control (P0 PASS) and then the inside-out turn of this session: the boundary-layer
dimension instrument (validated), the shadow-gravity reconciliation (all three
LeSage killers), the one-substrate synthesis — and the P1d recon that says, honestly,
the growth engine does not yet select geometry. **The mechanism is candidate-resolved
in toy models; the substrate-side wall is open.** The next real experiment is
dissipative growth measured with the validated instrument, under fresh
pre-registration.

## 8.6 The one honest sentence

A single operation on a raw graph, read inside-out, is a **candidate** for producing
gravity, perception, and dimension — demonstrated in toy models and defended against
an adversarial skeptic — but it has **not** been shown to select a low-dimensional
geometry in the first place, and it has **zero** real-world experimental validation.
