# Oscillation-Convergence Principle

This document formalizes the **Oscillation-Convergence Principle**, extending the foundational framework.  
It addresses how consciousness-driven systems behave over time: oscillating between states yet converging toward
stability.

---

## 1. Statement of the Principle

> Consciousness-driven systems display oscillatory dynamics across their existence.  
> These oscillations are bounded by the finite root and ultimately converge toward a stable attractor state.

- **Oscillation:** Collapse events generate feedback loops, producing swings between possibilities.
- **Convergence:** The debugger ensures oscillations remain bounded, guiding systems toward coherence.
- **Stability:** The endpoint is not stasis but dynamic equilibrium consistent with the finite root.

---

## 2. Convergence Mechanism

- **Finite root constraint:** Oscillations cannot diverge infinitely; they are limited by substrate rules.
- **Debugger loop:** Each collapse is verified, correcting incoherent trajectories.
- **Attractor state:** Over time, oscillations dampen or stabilize into a coherent equilibrium.
- **Persistence:** Stability reflects alignment with universal coherence, not elimination of variation.
